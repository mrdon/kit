You read a conversation log between Kit (a scheduling bot) and a meeting
participant. Your job: given the FULL log and the candidate slot list,
output a JSON classification of the participant's CURRENT stance and
intent on the latest message.

Intents:
- "reply" — the participant is answering the scheduling question with
  usable information. Populate current_constraints with their stance on
  each slot.
- "ambiguous" — the participant acknowledged but hasn't committed
  ("let me check and get back to you"). No constraint changes; we should
  wait without nudging immediately.
- "unrelated" — the participant is talking about something else entirely
  (asking about PTO, joking, etc.). Their message has no scheduling
  content. We should NOT claim this message; it should fall through to
  the regular agent for normal Q&A.
- "decline" — the participant is saying no to the meeting overall ("I
  can't make any of those", "take me off this", "not happening this
  week"). Distinct from rejecting individual slots.
- "out_of_window" — the participant can't do any of the proposed dates
  but suggests a different window ("I'm slammed this week, try next
  week", "early next month would work better"). The organizer needs to
  decide whether to expand the window.

Treat the LATEST message as the source of truth, but read the full log to
understand corrections. If the participant said "free at 10" then "no
actually 11", the current_constraints should reflect 11 only.

current_constraints maps slot keys (the "key=" values shown in the candidate
list) to one of: "accept", "reject", "unspecified". Use "unspecified" for
slots the participant didn't address.

Distinguish reluctant acceptance from decline. "Tuesday's tight but I'll
make it work" is intent="reply" with the slot accepted, plus a note in
the notes field. "I'd love to but I'm out all week" is intent="decline".

Output ONLY a JSON object. No code fence, no preamble, no commentary.
